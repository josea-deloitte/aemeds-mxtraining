What is VYEPTI?

VYEPTI® (eptinezumab-jjmr) 100 mg/mL Injection for IV is a prescription medicine used for the preventive treatment of migraine in adults. It is given as an intravenous (IV) infusion by a healthcare provider every 3 months. VYEPTI works by blocking a protein called calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP), which is believed to play a role in migraine attacks.

Clinical trials showed that VYEPTI significantly reduced the number of migraine days per month compared with placebo. Patients receiving 100 mg or 300 mg doses experienced meaningful reductions in monthly migraine days starting from the first week after the first infusion.

VYEPTI is administered by a healthcare provider in a clinical setting as a 30-minute IV infusion. The recommended dose is 100 mg administered once every 3 months. A 300 mg dose, administered once every 3 months, may be warranted for some patients.

As with all prescription medicines, patients should discuss the risks and benefits of VYEPTI with their healthcare provider. See full Prescribing Information and Patient Information below.

Clinical Trial Results

VYEPTI was studied in two pivotal clinical trials: PROMISE 1 (episodic migraine) and PROMISE 2 (chronic migraine). Both trials demonstrated statistically significant reductions in monthly migraine days compared to placebo.

In PROMISE 1, patients with episodic migraine (4–14 migraine days/month) receiving VYEPTI 100 mg had a mean reduction of 3.9 migraine days per month, compared with 3.2 days for placebo — a statistically significant difference. Patients on 300 mg had a mean reduction of 4.3 migraine days.

In PROMISE 2, patients with chronic migraine (≥15 headache days/month, ≥8 of which were migraine days) receiving VYEPTI 100 mg had a mean reduction of 7.7 migraine days per month, compared with 5.6 for placebo. The 300 mg arm showed a mean reduction of 8.2 migraine days per month.

In both studies, VYEPTI demonstrated a rapid onset of action, with differences from placebo apparent during the first week following the first infusion. The most common side effects were nasopharyngitis (stuffy nose and scratchy throat) and hypersensitivity reactions.

These results highlight the value of VYEPTI as a preventive treatment option for patients who experience frequent migraines and may not have responded well to other preventive therapies.

APPROVED USE

VYEPTI is a prescription medicine used for the preventive treatment of migraine in adults.

IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION

Do not receive VYEPTI if you have a known allergy to eptinezumab-jjmr or its ingredients.

VYEPTI may cause serious side effects such as:

  • Allergic reactions. Call your healthcare provider or get emergency medical help right away if you have any symptoms of an allergic reaction: rash; swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat; if you have trouble breathing; hives; or redness in your face.
  • High blood pressure. High blood pressure or worsening of high blood pressure can happen after receiving VYEPTI. Contact your healthcare provider if you have an increase in blood pressure.
  • Raynaud’s phenomenon. A type of circulation problem (Raynaud’s phenomenon) can worsen or happen after receiving VYEPTI. Contact your healthcare provider if you have symptoms such as your fingers or toes feeling numb, cool, or painful, or changing color from pale to blue to red.

Before starting VYEPTI, tell your healthcare provider about all your medical conditions, including if you have high blood pressure; circulation problems in your fingers and toes; are pregnant or plan to become pregnant; or are breastfeeding or plan to breastfeed.

Tell your healthcare provider about all the medicines you take, including any prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, or herbal supplements.

The most common side effects of VYEPTI include stuffy nose and scratchy throat, and allergic reactions. These are not all the possible side effects of VYEPTI. Tell your healthcare provider if you have any side effect that bothers you or that does not go away.

You are encouraged to report negative side effects of prescription drugs to the FDA. Visit www.fda.gov/medwatch or call 1-800-FDA-1088.
